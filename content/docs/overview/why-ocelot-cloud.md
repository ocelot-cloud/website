Citizens and small businesses rely on cloud, messaging, and video calling every day, but no single service meets all their needs:

* convenience
* privacy 
* customization
* long-term security

Let's look at some typical approaches, why they fall short, and what the ideal solution looks like.

### Option 1: External Cloud Services

Outsourcing to an external cloud is hassle-free, no hardware, pay-as-you-go, but it comes with serious trade-offs:

* Unpredictable terms and conditions: sudden price increases, feature removals, even bankruptcy.
* Vendor lock-in: Migration can be slow, costly, or technically painful.
* Opaque infrastructure: data resides in unknown locations and may be subject to unauthorized inspection.
* Limited freedom: Proprietary software are closed off limiting customization and integration.

**Takeaway: To maintain control of our data, we need self-hosted infrastructure.**

### Option 2: Do-It-Yourself Hosting

Running your own digital infrastructure gives you full control, but requires deep system administration expertise, knowledge of each installed software and its specifics, constant updating, backups, etc., which is typically out of reach for most individuals and SMBs.

**Takeaway: Automation must replace manual maintenance.**

### Option 3: Proprietary Self-Hosting Suites

There are proprietary solutions, i.e. their source code is not open, which brings back the same problems as with cloud providers: vendor lock-in, unilateral license changes, etc.

**Takeaway: Freedom requires open-source.**

### Solution: Enter Ocelot-Cloud

* **Open-source Community Edition** — all essential features at no cost, as well as the freedoms to adapt and share.
* **Automated upkeep** — updates and backups handled for you.
* **Self-hosting freedom** — deploy on hardware you control.
* **No-lock-in Premium Edition** — optional advanced features that fund development; switch editions or migrate servers anytime.
* **Open App Store** — anyone can contribute and install community apps for a diverse ecosystem.
